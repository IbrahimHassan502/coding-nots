choosing colors

of course any color you make you add it in your style as a variable so when you need to modify it you do it from one place

first you start by choosing your primary color, you can pick it either:

  • by the website vibes using the color phsycology
  • or from a provided theme
  • or from the logo
  • or from the hero section (on your own or using the theme extract in adobe color)

then you choose your secondary color either by knowing color harmony or using one of these color picking websites and also read the HSB color model

then make a gradient from the main and the secondary color (in case you need it)

then you choose your text color which the instructor in envato tuts+ course called the greys as he made a main black color and made different shades of it but the text color doesn't have to be black (in case of dark theme the text is a light color)

as for the black shades the instructor prefers to make a black from his primary color

and don't use pure black (or white probably) as it provides too much contrast for the eye

or you can put a black overlay over your primary color and reduce the overlay opacity until you get a good tone

then he made the different shades by reducing it's opacity to 75%, 50%, 25%, 10%, 5%

then choose an error message (in case you have elements like forms) which should be a complemntary redish color to your primary one

after choosing your colors don't forget to check on the contrast you can use figma plugins like contrast or stark

color phsycology general guide lines

  • red for passion, grabing attention, getting the message across, intensity, energy, excitement

    often used in sport related websites or websites that want to make a bold statement

  • blue for trust, calm, peace, intelligenece, security

    often used by tech brands like facebook and twitter, financial instituations, political parties

  • yellow for fun, bold, joy, happiness, energy

    used in entrtainment industry and children's toys

  • green for health, welbeing, fertilty, balance, saftey and also used with anything related to landscaping and gardening
  • orange for happiness, joy, confidenece, energy, optimizm, communication and it's complementary to blue
  • purple for luxury, royality, wealth, sophistication

HSB color model

it stands for hue saturation brightness

hue is basically the color itself then saturation is how vibrant (pure) or dull (greyish) the color is and birghtness is how bright (whitish) or dim (blackish) the color is

hue varies from 0 to 360 with every number preseneting a different color

where brightness and saturation vary from 0 to 100

so by varying the saturation you are changin the place of the color point in the color picker horizontally, meaning that a 50 saturation and 100 brightness will but the point exactly at the middle top of the picker

where a 50 saturation with 50 brightness will put in the center of the picker

typography

envato tuts course didn't put specific steps to detect the right font

basically you have to understand the brand and it's end users (and in my opinion see a lot of websites in the field you're designing for)

for example luxurious brands use serif fonts, and tech brands use sans serif fonts to give a feeling of modern and trust

the following is from my experience: as a rule of thumb you shouldn't use more than 2 font families in your website as more than that affects the performance and consistency of the website

after choosing your fonts you set the rule of each font: which gonna be used for headings, which gonna be used for paragraphs, etc...

finally don't forget to check for it's readability (by reducing it's font size down to the smalles value you will be using and see how it does)

now after choosing your fonts you choose their attributes (weights, fontsizes, scale for the headings, line heights)

as for the base font size envato tuts instructor chose 21 explaining that it's a bit larger than usual but that what suits the exact design he was working on in the course and didn't say exact steps on how to choose the base so I made my research

according to learn ui web pages can be categorized into two categories based on the text

  • text-heavy pages: basically any page you keep reading in like (documentations and articles) the main function of the page for you is to read

    examples: medium.com or tailwind documentation

  • interaction-heavy pages: apps that require alot of clicking, searching and looking for items

    examples: facebook, and instagram

so the general rule is to never go less than 16px one reason for that is inputs with 16px ios browsers will automatically zoom in on the left side of them making having to zoom out manually to conintue browsing normally creating an awful user experience in my opinion

one exception for that rule is when the text is "less important" like a comment on a sentence you can make it 14px or 15px (iphone use 17px base and 15px less important)

so when it comes to text-heavy web pages it's good to use a bigger font size than 16px something like 18px or even 20px (medium.com عمنا وعم المجال uses 20px )

as for interaction-heavy web pages 16px is a very good spot (facebook uses 15px, and insta uses 14px)

according to medium for desktop (1440 x 1024 px down to 1024 x 768 px) text-heavy pages as for desktop interaction-heavy pages
after choosing your base fontsize go to typescale.com or archetypeapp.com/ to get your headings fontsizes based on your base fontsize and the scale you prefere

finally when you're all done with the fontsizes add them as styles in figma

terminology

tint

tint is when you add white to the colors

and this is achievable by moving the color point in the color picker horizontally to the right (towards the white)

shading

a shade is when you add black to the color

and you ahcieve that by moving the color point vertically downwards (towards the black)

tone

a tone is when you add gery to the color and it is achieved by moving the color point diagonally in the color picker

variable colors

why use variable colors? simply as it makes it way easier to modify the color scheme of the design saying you have primary and secondary colors you don't have to go change every element that has the changed color by yourself you just changed once it you're ready to go

how to make a variable color

  1. select any element you want to assign the color to
  2. find the fill in the design bar on the right
  3. click on the four dots on the right of the fill and hit the plus icon
  4. type the name and description of the color as instructed in the placeholder

    notice the plus icon on highlighted by the rose rectangular it lets you add shades or something like that I didn't experment with it yet

how to use variable colors

  1. after selecting the element you want to assign the color to under fill on the design bar on the right click on the color square to pick a color
  2. on the top of the new panel click on libraries then choose your color

make varients of variable color

let's say you have a button with the main color and you want to make a varient of it with a darker color in this case you have to break it's link to the main color otherwise you'll be changing the main color itself

you break the link by clicking the broken chain loop beside the color name

components

when to use components

  • when making an animated element like a spiner
  • when making an interacting element like button with hover state
  • when repeating the same element with changes like pricing cards or navbar links

compenets with repeated elements

it's really important espicially with something like navbar links so when you need to make a change like the color or the casing of the letters you just change the parent component and all of them change instead of you going for one by one (just like classes in css)

how to make a component and varient

lets say we're making a navbar element. You want to make three states

  1. the active state (usually home)
  2. the default one
  3. and the hover state

  • the the best practice that you make a seperate component page

    to make a new page click on the "page-1" on the right side of left bar

    here I renamed it to "final design" but in default it says "page-1"

    once you click on it, it will open a list of all the pages you have click the plus icon to add a page (figma allows 3 pages per project in the free plan)

  • go to your component page, choose the type tool either from the top bar or by hitting "t" on the keyboard, then type the first navbar link you want which is usually "home"
  • select that element and from the top bar hit the diamond-like icon
  • name your component from the right bar under current varient
  • make varient by selecting the component then in the place of the diamond-like icon you will find a plus icon looks like it when you click that it makes you a new varient, make the changes you want

  • then to make the hover state make a third varient with the changes you like then from the right open the prototype

    hit the plus icon from the on click dropdown choose while hovering then hover over the default state varient, a small plus icon will show up drag from the icon then connect to the last varient which is the hover state

    from the instant dropdown menu choose smart animation

  • now to use the component in the design go to the main design page from the left bar choose assests

    then drag the component and drop it in the design

  • to choose between the different variations from the right bar under the name of the component (which in our case called nav-item) click on the dropdown menu on the right of "property 1" and choose the name or the state you want

  • to modify the compenent you don't modify the component instant in the main design you go to the main component in the component page and modify that

    note that in that crash course the instructor made the changes for the three varients one by one, so I'm not sure if that's the only way or there's a way to make changes just once and it applies for the three of them

  • if you want to test the hover animatino now click on the play button on the top right of the top bar

text baseline alignment

sometimes when you align text with an icon the alignment is a bit off (even if you make sure they're centered)

to solve this go to the advanced settings in autolayout and click on the check mark next to align text baseline

padding and spacing

when you hover on the edges of the autolayout a pink handle appears (for the padding) or when you hover between the items (for the spacing)

you can either drag the handle and change the padidng or the spacing or you can click it and enter a specific number

if you want to change the padding on all directions at one time hold shift + alt while dragging or clicking the padding handle

or you can enter the padding values from the right bar, you will find the top&bottom value and the left&right value if you want to enter different value (for top different than bottom for exmple) you can either click the icon on their right and it will show you 4 values value for each side or you can enter you values seprated by commas like css (12, 14 for example)

if you want to enter a specific value for all side from the right bar hold ctrl and click on any value and enter the value you want

or you can enter four values separated by commas when you hold ctrl and click any of the values

to position an item absolutly

  1. add it the auto layout (don't panick if it misses up the layout)
  2. select the item then click the absolute position icon on the right bar next to the x and y

to toggle between packed and spaced mode in distributing auto layout you can either click the spacing box and hit x on your keyboard or you can click the small arrow on the right of the gap number and choose auto

include border / strokes

by default bordes are not included in the auto layout sizing to change that go to the advanced settings of the auto layout by clicking the three dots icon on the right in the auto layout panel in the right bar and next to strokes choose include from the dropdown menu
